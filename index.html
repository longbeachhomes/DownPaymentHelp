SAY WHAT?
A DOWN PAYMENT?

For many, putting together a down payment to buy a house is the most difficult part in the home buying process. The National Association of Realtors and RealtyTrac say that the time needed for a first-time homebuyer to collect a 20% of the house purchase price would be approximately 12.5 years, based on the rate of savings of a typical person. And for many more, even saving for the 3.5% required in a government backed loan appears as a daunting task.

THERE IS MONEY AVAILABLE

A recently released report says that 70% of first time home buyers (and current home owners) in California are not aware that the Federal and State government agencies offer millions of dollars in down payment assistance or closing cost assistance programs that go unused each year.

NOT A FIRST TIMER?

There are more than 260 homebuyer assistance programs in California, and approximately 37% of them DO NOT require you to be a first time buyer.

About Down Payment Assistance (DPA)

Where Can I Find Downpayment Assistance Programs? Homebuyer assistance is usually offered by government agencies at the federal, state, county, and city level. There are also some non-profit or government public agencies offering DP assistance.

Is it difficult to Qualify for Down Payment Assistance? Each program and sponsoring agency has its own qualification criteria, but the most important of them are your income (you have to earn less than certain limit) and your credit score which for some of the programs is a minimum of 620 and for others 640, other than that you are likely to qualify!
